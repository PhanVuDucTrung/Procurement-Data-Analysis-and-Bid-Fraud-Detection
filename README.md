# Procurement-Data-Analysis-and-Bid-Fraud-Detection
Anomaly Detection in Bidding: Identifying Bidding Collusion in the Kentucky School Milk Market

## Abstract
This data analysis project investigated potential bidding fraud and collusion in the Kentucky school milk market over a nine-year period. Using a **data-driven approach**, the analysis compared a suspected collusion market (Tri-County) with a competitive benchmark (Surround).
The project's goal was to proactively identify operational vulnerabilities in supply chain contracts, visualize price anomalies, and generate actionable insights to optimize bidding costs and ensure fair competition.

## Key Technologies and Skills

- Tools: Microsoft Excel (Advanced), Power Query, Data Visualization.
- Analytical Skills: Statistical Analysis (Standard Deviation, Correlation), Data Storytelling.

## Key Actionable Insights
Through interactive performance dashboards and historical data analysis, four serious operational flaws have been identified:
+ Market Allocation: The rigid 50-50 supplier split and extremely high repeat bid win rate (>90%) in the Tri-County area indicate a severe lack of competition compared to a dynamic benchmark market.
+ Pricing Behavior: Unlike the Surround area (r=0.514), pricing in Tri-County completely ignores material cost volatility (r=0.049), clearly suggesting pre-agreed pricing.
+ Bid Volatility: The unusually low bid volatility in Tri-County suggests an artificially controlled environment where suppliers coordinate pricing instead of competing.
+ Financial Impact: The lack of competitive bidding forced Tri-County to pay a significant premium, substantially increasing overall procurement costs.
